Founder


Identifies a problem or opportunity in the market. Develops a vision or product idea. Sets the long-term direction for the company. Finds early customers or users. Builds partnerships with other companies or service providers. Helps define go-to-market strategies. Shifts from hands-on tasks to strategic leadership

CEO


Defines the long-term mission and strategic goals. Manages senior leaders. Hires, evaluates, and mentors top-level managers. Aligns the team around a clear roadmap.Approves large investments, product pivots, and hiring plans. Signs off on acquisitions, partnerships, or company expansions. Balances risks and returns.

MD


Takes full responsibility for running the business. Drives execution of the company’s strategy, goals, and vision. Ensures day-to-day operations are aligned with long-term objectives. Works with the CFO to manage the company budget, profitability, and cash flow. Builds trust and maintains transparency.

Manager


Manages a team of developers, designers, testers, or other specialists. Assigns tasks, sets goals, and tracks performance. Motivates the team and resolves conflicts. Acts as a bridge between technical teams and business teams. Reports updates to upper management. Identifies risks to delivery or performance early

HR


Writes job descriptions for roles like software engineers, testers, designers. Conducts initial screenings, schedules interviews, and manages the hiring process. managers for technical interviews. Manages salaries, bonuses, tax-related work. Handles insurance, leaves, holidays. Time and accurate salary payments.

Team Leader


Assigns tasks to team members based on skill and work. Keeps the team organized and motivated. Reviews code, provides technical guidance, and best practices. Works closely with Project Managers to plan and deliver tasks on time. Identifies high performers and areas for improvement. Solves technical issues and supports junior developers.

Employee


Work closely with team members (developers, testers, designers, etc.). Attend team meetings, stand-ups, sprint planning, etc. Communicate progress, blockers, and updates. Write clean, secure, maintainable code or create well-tested systems. Ensure work is delivered on time and meets specifications. Follow coding standards or design guidelines.

Course Member


Participate in live or recorded classes, online or offline. Learn theoretical concepts and practical applications. Write code or use IT tools based on what they’re learning. Complete assignments, labs, or exercises. Work on real-world projects. Some course members aim for certifications. Others prepare for job interviews, internships, or entry-level IT roles.

InternShip Member


Help full-time employees with actual coding, testing, or documentation. Work on small modules or support tasks. Shadow senior developers or team leads to learn the workflow. Work on assigned mini-projects or tasks that align with the team's work. Learn the workflow of an IT company: from idea to release. Experience what a real tech job is like.
